World’s Columbian Exposition Elongated Coin

front (left) and back (right) of Elongated Coin

c. 1893 - Chicago, IL

photo courtesy of the Numismatic Guaranty Company

Flat, shiny, and new; this original elongated coin from the 1893 World’s Columbian Exposition would have been a never before seen piece of memorabilia for a 19th century visitor. Today, we know that these are coins that have been rolled under enough pressure to flatten them out and press a design into their shiny skin. Back then, they represented the new technology that was debuting in droves at the turn of the century.

Notice how it doesnt look like a penny? That's because it's not! Original elongated coins were made out of “V” nickels. This didnt last though. By 1901, pennies became the preferred coin due to their malleable makeup.
